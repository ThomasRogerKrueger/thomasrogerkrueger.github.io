What I Do

As a digital storyteller, my skills encompass both the digital realm and the physical world. I use thorough research and creative thinking to overcome challenges and deliver high-quality imagery to display the themes and messages desired. Below are samples of my work in both a personal and professional space.

Walsworth Career Fair Redesign

In the summer of 2019, I was working as a sales recruiting intern in the corporate office of Walsworth Publishing. While working there, I was asked to create new signage for use at college career fairs. I created multiple banners of different sizes for their recruiting department. This project allowed me to dig deep into a business’ image and create designs that displayed these qualities. The work is easily customizable and transportable, as well as shows the important aspects of the position Walsworth was recruiting for. I learned much about the creative process within a company setting and pitching concepts to executives. Unfortunately, my work computer was not strong enough to efficiently run Adobe Photoshop, so this was created on Canva.

Family Farm

his is a short documentary I shot by myself in rural Missouri. The documentary follows a day in the life of a father-son farming operation as they rush to complete a day’s work before the coming winter. This documentary showed me the ropes in creating a long project from scratch with only myself. I am satisfied with the composition of the shots, but the audio could contain more life and a better mix of dialogue and diegetic sounds. If I could go back and reshoot, I would take more care in the capturing of sound and the discussions the farmers had.

Shot with a Canon DSLR camera, DJI Osmo, Rode shotgun microphone, 85 mm lens, and a 50 mm lens.

Cooking PSA

I was tasked with creating a relevant and interesting PSA that would appeal to a chosen audience. I chose to direct this PSA to young, working professionals who often choose takeout rather than a home-cooked meal. By showing a high quality yet simple meal, I strive to communicate an uplifting and encouraging message.Through careful research of the target audience, I discovered that younger professionals feel as though they do not have the time to cook a meal from home. Through careful recipe selection with simple and familiar ingredients, I effectively shot and edited this PSA by myself. The pacing is a bit frantic in my opinion and would have liked to slowed down the action and really sit in the imagery more.

Created with a Canon DSLR and a 50 mm lens. Edited with Adobe Premiere and Adobe Audition.
